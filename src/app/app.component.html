> How it works

> Cipher: Shift given text by the given k factor.

> Provide Reference: Provide a reference text body in your language of choice. The app will calculate and remember the character frequency distribution.

> Decipher: Provide a ciphered text. For all possible k values from -800 to 800, the app will calculate the character frequency distribution and compare it to the reference frequency distribtion. The k value that yields the closest frequency distribution is the negative of the original k used in ciphering.

Character frequency distributions A and B are compared using the following algorithm.
Ensure that A and B are normalized. Sum up cell-wise differences. Heavily penalize non-common characters,
(because it is highly unlikely that two large bodies of text in the same language have many non-common characters.)
Do not ignore the space character. The ratio of spacebar to each character is an important characteristic of a language. A language with longer words will have a lower freq for the space character.

  1. Start with total_error = 0.
  2. Let U := the union of all characters in A and B.
  3. For each character c in U:
    abs_err := Math.abs(A[c] - B[c]).
    err := abs_err.
    if c is NOT a common character in A and B: err = err * large_miss_penalty.
    total_error = total_error + err.
